# MSA Template Structure

Master Services Agreement for professional and consulting services with Statement of Work framework.

---

## Document Header

```
MASTER SERVICES AGREEMENT

Effective Date: [DATE]
Agreement Number: [MSA-XXXX]
```

---

## Section 1: Parties

```
This Master Services Agreement ("Agreement") is between:

SERVICE PROVIDER:
Name: [FULL LEGAL NAME]
Address: [REGISTERED ADDRESS]
Registration Number: [COMPANY NUMBER]
("Provider")

CLIENT:
Name: [FULL LEGAL NAME]
Address: [REGISTERED ADDRESS]
Registration Number: [COMPANY NUMBER]
("Client")

Each a "Party" and together the "Parties".
```

---

## Section 2: Definitions

```
In this Agreement:

"Affiliate" means any entity that directly or indirectly controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with a Party, where "control" means ownership of more than 50% of voting securities.

"Background IP" means any Intellectual Property owned by or licensed to a Party prior to this Agreement, or developed by a Party independently of this Agreement.

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by one Party to the other, whether orally, in writing, or in any other form, that is designated as confidential or would reasonably be understood to be confidential.

"Deliverables" means the tangible and intangible work product, materials, and deliverables to be provided by Provider as specified in a Statement of Work.

"Intellectual Property" or "IP" means patents, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets, know-how, and all other intellectual property rights.

"Services" means the professional services to be provided by Provider as described in this Agreement and each Statement of Work.

"Statement of Work" or "SOW" means a document executed by both Parties that describes specific Services, Deliverables, fees, timelines, and other project-specific terms, substantially in the form of Exhibit A.

"Work Product" means all Deliverables, inventions, works of authorship, and other materials created by Provider specifically for Client in performing the Services.
```

## Section 3: Services and Statements of Work

```
3.1 Services
Provider will perform the Services described in each Statement of Work executed by the Parties. Each SOW will specify:
(a) Description of Services and Deliverables;
(b) Project timeline and milestones (if applicable);
(c) Fees and payment terms;
(d) Acceptance criteria (if applicable);
(e) Any project-specific terms that supplement this Agreement.

3.2 Incorporation
Each SOW is incorporated into and governed by this Agreement. In the event of a conflict between this Agreement and a SOW, the SOW will prevail with respect to the Services described in that SOW, unless the SOW expressly states otherwise.

3.3 Change Orders
Either Party may request changes to a SOW. No change is binding unless documented in a written change order signed by both Parties, specifying the scope change, fee adjustment, and timeline impact.

3.4 Performance Standards
Provider will perform the Services:
(a) In a professional and workmanlike manner;
(b) In accordance with applicable industry standards;
(c) Using personnel with appropriate skills and experience;
(d) In compliance with all applicable laws and regulations.

3.5 Client Responsibilities
Client will:
(a) Provide timely access to information, personnel, and resources reasonably required for Provider to perform the Services;
(b) Make decisions and provide approvals in a timely manner;
(c) Notify Provider promptly of any issues or concerns;
(d) Comply with its obligations as specified in each SOW.
```

## Section 4: Fees and Payment

*Select and customize based on payment model:*

### Option A: Time & Materials

```
4.1 Fees
Client will pay Provider for Services at the rates specified in the applicable SOW. Unless otherwise specified:
(a) Rates are quoted in [USD/GBP/EUR];
(b) Time is billed in increments of [0.25/0.5/1] hour;
(c) Rates may be adjusted annually upon [30/60/90] days' written notice.

4.2 Expenses
Client will reimburse Provider for reasonable, pre-approved expenses incurred in performing the Services, including:
(a) Travel expenses (economy class airfare, standard hotel, ground transportation);
(b) Third-party costs directly related to the Services.
Expenses exceeding [AMOUNT] require prior written approval. Provider will submit expense documentation with each invoice.

4.3 Invoicing
Provider will submit invoices [monthly/upon milestone completion] detailing:
(a) Hours worked by personnel and applicable rates;
(b) Description of Services performed;
(c) Itemized expenses with receipts.

4.4 Payment Terms
Client will pay undisputed invoices within [30] days of receipt. Disputed amounts must be identified in writing within [15] days of invoice receipt, with reasonable detail.

4.5 Late Payment
Overdue amounts bear interest at [1.0/1.5]% per month (or the maximum rate permitted by law, if lower). Provider may suspend Services upon [15] days' notice if payment is more than [30] days overdue.
```

```

(c) [X]% due upon final acceptance.

4.3 Scope Changes
Fixed fees apply only to the scope defined in the SOW. Any scope changes require a written change order with adjusted fees.

4.4 Invoicing
Provider will submit invoices in accordance with the payment schedule in each SOW.

4.5 Payment Terms
Client will pay undisputed invoices within [30] days of receipt.

4.6 Late Payment

### Option C: Milestone-Based

```
4.1 Milestone Payments
Client will pay Provider upon achievement and acceptance of milestones as specified in each SOW.

4.2 Acceptance Process
(a) Provider will notify Client upon completion of each milestone;
(b) Client will review and either accept or provide written rejection with specific deficiencies within [10/15] business days;
(c) If Client fails to respond within the review period, the milestone is deemed accepted;
(d) Provider will address legitimate deficiencies and resubmit within [X] days;
(e) Payment is due within [30] days of acceptance.

4.3 Partial Completion
If this Agreement or a SOW is terminated before completion, Client will pay for:
(a) All accepted milestones;
(b) Pro-rata portion of work completed toward the next milestone.
```

```

4.2 Overage

4.3 Rollover
[Unused hours expire at month end / Up to X unused hours may roll over to the following month].

4.4 Invoicing
Provider will invoice on the [1st/15th] of each month in advance. Usage reports will be provided [monthly/quarterly].

## Section 5: Term and Termination

```
5.1 Term
This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for an initial term of [1/2/3] year(s), unless terminated earlier in accordance with this Section. Thereafter, this Agreement will automatically renew for successive [1]-year periods unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least [60/90] days before the end of the then-current term.

5.2 Termination for Convenience
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience upon [30/60] days' written notice to the other Party.

5.3 Termination for Cause
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party:
(a) Materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within [30] days of written notice specifying the breach;
(b) Becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or ceases to operate in the ordinary course.

5.4 Effect of Termination
Upon termination or expiration:
(a) Client will pay Provider for all Services performed and expenses incurred through the termination date;
(b) Each Party will return or destroy the other's Confidential Information;
(c) Provider will deliver all completed or in-progress Deliverables (upon payment);
(d) Sections [specify surviving sections] will survive termination.

5.5 Wind-Down
Upon termination for convenience by Client, Provider will:
(a) Cease work in an orderly manner;
(b) Cooperate with transition to Client or a successor provider;
(c) Complete any wind-down activities specified in the applicable SOW.
```

## Section 6: Intellectual Property

```
6.1 Background IP
Each Party retains all rights in its Background IP. Neither Party grants the other any rights in its Background IP except as expressly provided in this Agreement.

6.2 Work Product Ownership

[OPTION A - CLIENT OWNS (Work for Hire)]
All Work Product created by Provider specifically for Client under this Agreement will be owned by Client. To the extent any Work Product does not qualify as "work made for hire" under applicable law, Provider hereby assigns to Client all right, title, and interest in such Work Product. Provider will execute any documents reasonably necessary to perfect Client's ownership.

[OPTION B - PROVIDER OWNS WITH LICENSE]
Provider retains all right, title, and interest in the Work Product. Provider grants Client a non-exclusive, perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free license to use, copy, modify, and create derivative works of the Work Product for Client's internal business purposes.

[OPTION C - SHARED/JOINT]
The Parties will jointly own the Work Product, with each Party having the right to use, license, and exploit the Work Product without accounting to the other Party, subject to the confidentiality obligations in this Agreement.

6.3 Provider Tools and Methods
Notwithstanding Section 6.2, Provider retains all rights in:
(a) Provider's pre-existing tools, methodologies, frameworks, and know-how;
(b) Generic, non-Client-specific improvements or developments.
Provider grants Client a non-exclusive, perpetual license to use any such materials solely as incorporated in the Deliverables.

6.4 Client Materials
Client retains all rights in materials provided by Client to Provider. Client grants Provider a limited license to use Client materials solely as necessary to perform the Services.

6.5 Third-Party Materials
If the Deliverables incorporate third-party materials, Provider will identify such materials and ensure Client receives appropriate licenses.
```

## Section 7: Confidentiality

```
7.1 Confidentiality Obligations
Each Party (as "Receiving Party") will:
(a) Hold the other Party's (as "Disclosing Party") Confidential Information in strict confidence;
(b) Use Confidential Information only for purposes of this Agreement;
(c) Disclose Confidential Information only to employees, contractors, and advisors who need to know and are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective;
(d) Protect Confidential Information using at least the same care as for its own confidential information, and no less than reasonable care.

7.2 Exclusions
Confidential Information does not include information that:
(a) Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party;
(b) Was known to the Receiving Party before disclosure;
(c) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of Confidential Information;
(d) Is lawfully received from a third party without restriction.

7.3 Permitted Disclosures
A Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information if required by law, provided it gives prompt notice (where permitted) and cooperates with efforts to obtain protective treatment.

7.4 Return of Information
Upon termination or request, each Party will return or destroy the other's Confidential Information, except for copies retained for legal compliance purposes.

7.5 Duration
Confidentiality obligations survive termination for [3/5] years, except for trade secrets which remain protected indefinitely.
```

## Section 8: Representations and Warranties

```
8.1 Mutual Warranties
Each Party represents and warrants that:
(a) It has the authority to enter into this Agreement;
(b) This Agreement constitutes a valid and binding obligation;
(c) It will comply with all applicable laws in performing its obligations.

8.2 Provider Warranties
Provider represents and warrants that:
(a) The Services will be performed in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards;
(b) Provider personnel have the skills, qualifications, and experience necessary to perform the Services;
(c) The Deliverables will substantially conform to the specifications in the applicable SOW;
(d) To Provider's knowledge, the Deliverables will not infringe any third party's Intellectual Property rights;
(e) Provider has the right to grant the licenses and assignments provided in this Agreement.

8.3 Warranty Remedy
If any Deliverable fails to conform to its warranty, Provider will, at its option:
(a) Re-perform the non-conforming Services; or
(b) Repair or replace the non-conforming Deliverable.
This remedy is Client's exclusive remedy for breach of the warranties in Section 8.2(c).

8.4 Warranty Period
Warranties on Deliverables apply for [60/90] days following delivery or acceptance.

8.5 Disclaimer
EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY PROVIDED IN THIS AGREEMENT, PROVIDER MAKES NO OTHER WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE, OR NON-INFRINGEMENT.
```

## Section 9: Indemnification

```
9.1 Provider Indemnification
Provider will indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Client and its Affiliates, and their officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any third-party claims, damages, losses, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from:
(a) Provider's gross negligence or willful misconduct;
(b) Provider's breach of the confidentiality obligations;
(c) Claims that the Deliverables infringe a third party's Intellectual Property rights.

9.2 Client Indemnification
Client will indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Provider and its Affiliates, and their officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any third-party claims, damages, losses, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from:
(a) Client's gross negligence or willful misconduct;
(b) Client's breach of the confidentiality obligations;
(c) Client's use of Deliverables in violation of this Agreement;
(d) Client materials that infringe a third party's Intellectual Property rights.

9.3 Indemnification Procedure
The indemnified party will:
(a) Promptly notify the indemnifying party of any claim;
(b) Give the indemnifying party sole control of defense and settlement;
(c) Provide reasonable cooperation at the indemnifying party's expense.
The indemnifying party will not settle any claim that imposes liability on the indemnified party without consent.

9.4 IP Infringement Remedies
If a Deliverable is subject to an infringement claim, Provider may, at its option:
(a) Procure the right for Client to continue using the Deliverable;
(b) Modify the Deliverable to be non-infringing; or
(c) Replace the Deliverable with a non-infringing equivalent.
If none of these options is commercially reasonable, either Party may terminate the affected SOW and Provider will refund fees paid for the infringing Deliverable.
```

## Section 10: Limitation of Liability

```
10.1 Exclusion of Consequential Damages
NEITHER PARTY WILL BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOSS OF PROFITS, REVENUE, DATA, OR BUSINESS OPPORTUNITY, REGARDLESS OF THE CAUSE OF ACTION OR WHETHER SUCH DAMAGES WERE FORESEEABLE.

10.2 Liability Cap

[OPTION A - 12 MONTHS FEES]
EXCEPT FOR THE EXCLUSIONS IN SECTION 10.3, EACH PARTY'S TOTAL AGGREGATE LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT WILL NOT EXCEED THE FEES PAID OR PAYABLE BY CLIENT IN THE [12] MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM.

[OPTION B - FEES UNDER SOW]
EXCEPT FOR THE EXCLUSIONS IN SECTION 10.3, EACH PARTY'S TOTAL AGGREGATE LIABILITY FOR CLAIMS ARISING FROM A PARTICULAR SOW WILL NOT EXCEED THE FEES PAID OR PAYABLE UNDER THAT SOW.

[OPTION C - FIXED AMOUNT]
EXCEPT FOR THE EXCLUSIONS IN SECTION 10.3, EACH PARTY'S TOTAL AGGREGATE LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT WILL NOT EXCEED [AMOUNT].

10.3 Exclusions from Cap
The limitations in Sections 10.1 and 10.2 do not apply to:
(a) Breaches of confidentiality obligations;
(b) Indemnification obligations under Section 9;
(c) Gross negligence or willful misconduct;
(d) Provider's infringement of Client's Intellectual Property;
(e) [Payment obligations / Personal injury or death caused by negligence].

10.4 Essential Purpose
THE PARTIES AGREE THAT THE LIMITATIONS IN THIS SECTION REFLECT A REASONABLE ALLOCATION OF RISK AND ARE A FUNDAMENTAL PART OF THE BASIS OF THE BARGAIN.
```

## Section 12: Independent Contractor

```
12.1 Relationship
Provider is an independent contractor. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship. Provider is solely responsible for determining the manner and means of performing the Services.

12.2 Personnel
Provider's personnel are not employees of Client. Provider is solely responsible for:
(a) Compensation, benefits, and payroll taxes for its personnel;
(b) Workers' compensation and unemployment insurance;
(c) Compliance with labor and employment laws.

12.3 No Authority
Neither Party has authority to bind the other or incur obligations on the other's behalf without prior written consent.
```

## Section 13: General Provisions

```
13.1 Entire Agreement
This Agreement, including all SOWs and Exhibits, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes all prior agreements, representations, and understandings regarding its subject matter.

13.2 Amendments
This Agreement may only be amended by a written document signed by both Parties.

13.3 Assignment
Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the other's prior written consent, except:
(a) To an Affiliate; or
(b) In connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all assets.
Any assignment in violation of this section is void.

13.4 Notices
All notices must be in writing and delivered to the addresses set forth above by:
(a) Personal delivery (effective upon delivery);
(b) Certified mail, return receipt requested (effective 5 days after mailing);
(c) Overnight courier (effective upon delivery); or
(d) Email (effective upon receipt confirmation, except for termination notices which require mail or courier).

13.5 Severability
If any provision is found invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions continue in effect. The Parties will negotiate in good faith to replace any invalid provision.

13.6 Waiver
No waiver of any right is effective unless in writing. Failure to enforce any right is not a waiver of that or any other right.

13.7 Force Majeure
Neither Party is liable for delays or failures caused by events beyond reasonable control, including natural disasters, war, terrorism, strikes, or government actions. The affected Party must notify the other promptly and use reasonable efforts to mitigate.

13.8 Governing Law
This Agreement is governed by the laws of [JURISDICTION], without regard to conflicts of law principles.

13.9 Dispute Resolution

[OPTION A - LITIGATION]
Any dispute arising from this Agreement will be resolved exclusively in the state and federal courts located in [JURISDICTION]. Each Party consents to personal jurisdiction and venue in such courts.

[OPTION B - ARBITRATION]
Any dispute arising from this Agreement will be resolved by binding arbitration under the [AAA/JAMS/ICC] Rules. Arbitration will be conducted in [CITY] by [one/three] arbitrator(s). The arbitrator's decision is final and binding. Judgment may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

13.10 Counterparts
This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, including electronic counterparts, each of which is an original.
```
