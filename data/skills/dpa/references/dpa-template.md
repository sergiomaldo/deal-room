# DPA Template Structure

Controller-to-Processor Data Processing Agreement compliant with EU GDPR, UK GDPR, and California CCPA/CPRA.

---

## Document Header

```
DATA PROCESSING AGREEMENT

Effective Date: [DATE]

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") forms part of the [MAIN AGREEMENT NAME, e.g., "Master Services Agreement"] dated [MAIN AGREEMENT DATE] (the "Principal Agreement") between the parties listed below.
```

## Section 2: Background and Scope

```
2.1 Background
The Controller has engaged the Processor to provide certain services under the Principal Agreement. In providing those services, the Processor will process Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.

2.2 Purpose
This DPA sets out the terms on which the Processor will process Personal Data on behalf of the Controller, as required by:
(a) Article 28 of the EU General Data Protection Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2016/679) ("EU GDPR");
(b) Article 28 of the UK General Data Protection Regulation as incorporated into UK law ("UK GDPR"); and
(c) The California Consumer Privacy Act of 2018 and the California Privacy Rights Act of 2020 ("CCPA/CPRA"), where applicable.

2.3 Scope of Processing
The Processor will process Personal Data only as described in Annex 1 (Details of Processing) and only for the purposes of providing the services under the Principal Agreement.
```

---

## Section 3: Definitions

```
In this DPA:

"Data Protection Laws" means all applicable laws relating to data protection and privacy, including:
(a) EU GDPR;
(b) UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018;
(c) CCPA/CPRA; and
(d) any other applicable national implementing legislation.

"Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person that the Processor processes on behalf of the Controller under the Principal Agreement.

"Processing" means any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, recording, organization, storage, adaptation, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.

"Personal Data Breach" means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data.

"Sub-processor" means any third party engaged by the Processor to process Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.

"Data Subject" means an identified or identifiable natural person whose Personal Data is processed.

"Supervisory Authority" means an independent public authority responsible for monitoring the application of Data Protection Laws.
```

---

## Section 4: Processor Obligations

```
4.1 Processing on Instructions
The Processor must:
(a) Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Controller, including with regard to transfers to third countries, unless required to do so by applicable law (in which case the Processor must inform the Controller of that legal requirement before processing, unless the law prohibits such notification);
(b) Immediately inform the Controller if, in the Processor's opinion, an instruction infringes Data Protection Laws.

4.2 Confidentiality
The Processor must ensure that all personnel authorized to process Personal Data:
(a) Have committed to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality; and
(b) Process Personal Data only as instructed by the Controller.

4.3 Security Measures
The Processor must implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect Personal Data against unauthorized or unlawful processing, accidental loss, destruction, or damage. These measures must:
(a) Be appropriate to the risk, taking into account the state of the art, costs of implementation, and the nature, scope, context, and purposes of processing;
(b) Include, as appropriate:
    (i) Pseudonymization and encryption of Personal Data;
    (ii) Ability to ensure ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience of processing systems;
    (iii) Ability to restore availability and access to Personal Data in a timely manner following an incident;
    (iv) Regular testing and evaluation of the effectiveness of security measures.
(c) Be described in Annex 2 (Technical and Organizational Measures).

4.4 Sub-processors

[OPTION A - GENERAL AUTHORIZATION]
(a) The Controller provides general authorization for the Processor to engage Sub-processors, subject to the requirements in this Section 4.4.
(b) The Processor must maintain a current list of Sub-processors, which is set out in Annex 3 (Approved Sub-processors) as of the Effective Date.
(c) The Processor must notify the Controller of any intended changes to Sub-processors at least 30 days before the change, giving the Controller an opportunity to object.
(d) If the Controller objects on reasonable grounds relating to data protection, the parties will discuss the objection in good faith. If they cannot resolve the objection, the Controller may terminate the affected services without penalty.

[OPTION B - SPECIFIC AUTHORIZATION]
(a) The Processor must not engage any Sub-processor without the Controller's prior specific written authorization.
(b) The Controller authorizes the Sub-processors listed in Annex 3 (Approved Sub-processors) as of the Effective Date.
(c) Any request to add or replace a Sub-processor must be submitted in writing and approved by the Controller before engagement.

[BOTH OPTIONS]
(e) When engaging any Sub-processor, the Processor must:
    (i) Carry out appropriate due diligence to ensure the Sub-processor can provide sufficient guarantees regarding data protection;
    (ii) Enter into a written contract with the Sub-processor that imposes data protection obligations equivalent to those in this DPA;
    (iii) Remain fully liable to the Controller for the Sub-processor's performance.

4.5 Assistance with Data Subject Rights
The Processor must:
(a) Taking into account the nature of the processing, assist the Controller by appropriate technical and organizational measures to respond to requests from Data Subjects exercising their rights under Data Protection Laws (including access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection);
(b) Promptly notify the Controller of any request received directly from a Data Subject, and not respond to that request except as authorized by the Controller or required by law.

4.6 Assistance with Compliance
The Processor must assist the Controller in ensuring compliance with the Controller's obligations regarding:
(a) Security of processing;
(b) Notification of Personal Data Breaches to supervisory authorities and Data Subjects;
(c) Data protection impact assessments; and
(d) Prior consultation with supervisory authorities.

4.7 Personal Data Breach Notification
In the event of a Personal Data Breach, the Processor must:
(a) Notify the Controller without undue delay, and in any event within [24/48/72] hours of becoming aware of the breach;
(b) Provide the Controller with sufficient information to enable the Controller to meet its obligations under Data Protection Laws, including:
    (i) Description of the nature of the breach, including categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and records concerned;
    (ii) Contact details of the Processor's data protection point of contact;
    (iii) Likely consequences of the breach;
    (iv) Measures taken or proposed to address the breach and mitigate its effects;
(c) Cooperate with the Controller and take reasonable steps as directed by the Controller to assist in the investigation, mitigation, and remediation of the breach.

4.8 Deletion and Return of Personal Data
Upon termination or expiration of the Principal Agreement, or upon the Controller's written request, the Processor must:
(a) At the Controller's choice, delete or return all Personal Data to the Controller;
(b) Delete existing copies unless applicable law requires retention;
(c) Complete deletion or return within [30/60/90] days;
(d) Certify in writing that it has complied with this Section 4.8.

4.9 Audit Rights
The Processor must:
(a) Make available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA and Article 28 of the GDPR;
(b) Allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by the Controller or an auditor mandated by the Controller, subject to:
    (i) Reasonable advance notice (at least 30 days, except in case of emergency or regulatory requirement);
    (ii) Reasonable scope and duration;
    (iii) Confidentiality obligations regarding any information obtained;
    (iv) The Processor may charge reasonable fees for audit support beyond annual audits.
```

## Section 5: Controller Obligations

```
5.1 The Controller warrants that:
(a) It has obtained all necessary consents, provided all necessary notices, and otherwise has a lawful basis to transfer Personal Data to the Processor for processing under this DPA;
(b) Its instructions to the Processor will comply with Data Protection Laws;
(c) It will provide the Processor with accurate and complete information regarding the processing.

5.2 The Controller is responsible for:
(a) Determining the purposes and means of processing Personal Data;
(b) Responding to Data Subject requests (with the Processor's assistance as set out above);
(c) Notifying supervisory authorities and Data Subjects of Personal Data Breaches as required by law.
```

---

## Section 6: International Data Transfers

```
6.1 Transfer Restrictions
The Processor must not transfer Personal Data to a country outside the European Economic Area ("EEA") or the United Kingdom ("UK") unless:
(a) The transfer is to a country that has been deemed to provide an adequate level of data protection by the European Commission or UK authorities (as applicable);
(b) Appropriate safeguards are in place as described in this Section 6; or
(c) A derogation under Article 49 of the GDPR applies.

6.2 Standard Contractual Clauses
Where Personal Data originating from the EEA is transferred to a country without an adequacy decision:
(a) The parties agree to be bound by the Standard Contractual Clauses adopted by the European Commission (Decision 2021/914), which are incorporated into this DPA by reference and attached as Annex 4;
(b) For Module Two (Controller to Processor) transfers, the parties agree that:
    (i) The Controller is the "data exporter";
    (ii) The Processor is the "data importer";
    (iii) The optional clauses and annexes are completed as set out in Annex 4.

6.3 UK International Data Transfer Addendum
Where Personal Data originating from the UK is transferred to a country without an adequacy decision:
(a) The parties agree to the UK International Data Transfer Addendum to the EU Standard Contractual Clauses, issued by the UK Information Commissioner;
(b) The Addendum is incorporated into this DPA and completed as set out in Annex 4.

6.4 Supplementary Measures
Where required by the circumstances of the transfer, the Processor must implement supplementary measures to ensure an essentially equivalent level of protection, which may include:
(a) Additional encryption in transit and at rest;
(b) Pseudonymization;
(c) Access controls and logging.
```

## Section 7: California-Specific Provisions (CCPA/CPRA)

```
7.1 Applicability
This Section 7 applies to the extent that the Processor processes Personal Information of California residents on behalf of the Controller.

7.2 Processor as Service Provider
For purposes of the CCPA/CPRA, the Processor is a "Service Provider." The Processor:
(a) Is prohibited from selling or sharing Personal Information;
(b) Is prohibited from retaining, using, or disclosing Personal Information for any purpose other than performing the services under the Principal Agreement, or as otherwise permitted by the CCPA/CPRA;
(c) Is prohibited from retaining, using, or disclosing Personal Information outside the direct business relationship between the Processor and Controller;
(d) Must comply with the CCPA/CPRA and provide the same level of privacy protection as required by the CCPA/CPRA;
(e) Must notify the Controller if it determines it can no longer meet its CCPA/CPRA obligations;
(f) Grants the Controller the right to take reasonable and appropriate steps to ensure the Processor uses Personal Information in a manner consistent with the Controller's obligations under the CCPA/CPRA;
(g) Grants the Controller the right to stop and remediate unauthorized use of Personal Information.

7.3 Certification
The Processor certifies that it understands and will comply with the restrictions in this Section 7.
```

---

## Section 8: Liability and Indemnification

```
8.1 Liability
Each party's liability under this DPA is subject to the limitations of liability set out in the Principal Agreement, except that:
(a) Neither party excludes or limits liability for breaches of Data Protection Laws to the extent such exclusion or limitation is prohibited by law;
(b) The Processor will be liable for damage caused by processing that violates Data Protection Laws or this DPA.

8.2 Indemnification
The Processor will indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Controller from and against any claims, damages, losses, costs, and expenses (including reasonable legal fees) arising from:
(a) The Processor's breach of this DPA;
(b) The Processor's violation of Data Protection Laws;
(c) Any act or omission of a Sub-processor engaged by the Processor.
```

---

## Section 9: Term and Termination

```
9.1 Term
This DPA commences on the Effective Date and continues until the Principal Agreement terminates or expires, or until all Personal Data is deleted or returned.

9.2 Survival
The obligations regarding deletion/return of Personal Data, confidentiality, and any provisions that by their nature should survive, will continue after termination.
```

---

## Section 10: General Provisions

```
10.1 Order of Precedence
In the event of a conflict between this DPA and the Principal Agreement, this DPA will prevail with respect to data protection matters.

10.2 Amendments
This DPA may only be amended in writing signed by both parties.

10.3 Governing Law
This DPA is governed by the laws of [JURISDICTION], without regard to conflicts of law principles.

10.4 Entire Agreement
This DPA, including its Annexes, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties regarding its subject matter.
```

- Encryption in transit (TLS 1.2 or higher)
- Encryption at rest (AES-256 or equivalent)
- Secure key management

3. Network Security
- Firewalls and intrusion detection/prevention
- Network segmentation
- Regular vulnerability scanning
- Penetration testing [FREQUENCY]

- Data centers with [CERTIFICATION, e.g., SOC 2, ISO 27001]

5. Personnel Security
- Background checks for personnel with data access
- Confidentiality agreements
- Regular security awareness training

6. Incident Response

- 24/7 security monitoring
- Regular testing of response procedures

7. Business Continuity
- Regular backups with [FREQUENCY]
