# NDA Template Structure

Use this structure when generating NDAs. Adapt jurisdiction-specific sections based on user selection.

---

## Document Header

```
MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

Effective Date: [DATE]
```

---

## Section 1: Parties

```
This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is between:

Party A:
Name: [FULL LEGAL NAME OF PARTY A]
Address: [REGISTERED ADDRESS]
Registration Number: [COMPANY NUMBER, IF APPLICABLE]
("Party A")

and

Party B:
Name: [FULL LEGAL NAME OF PARTY B]
Address: [REGISTERED ADDRESS]
Registration Number: [COMPANY NUMBER, IF APPLICABLE]
("Party B")

Each a "Party" and together the "Parties".
```

---

## Section 2: Background

```
The Parties wish to explore a potential business relationship concerning [BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF PURPOSE, e.g., "a potential joint venture in the technology sector"].

To evaluate this opportunity, each Party may need to share confidential information with the other. This Agreement sets out how both Parties must protect that information.
```

---

## Section 3: Definitions

```
In this Agreement:

"Confidential Information" means any information disclosed by one Party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other Party (the "Receiving Party"), whether orally, in writing, or in any other form, that:
(a) is marked as "confidential" or "proprietary"; or
(b) would reasonably be understood to be confidential given its nature or the circumstances of disclosure.

This includes, but is not limited to: business plans, financial information, customer lists, technical data, trade secrets, product designs, marketing strategies, and any other commercially sensitive information.

"Representatives" means a Party's employees, directors, officers, advisors, consultants, and agents who need access to Confidential Information for the Purpose.

"Purpose" means [DESCRIBE THE SPECIFIC PURPOSE, e.g., "evaluating and negotiating a potential business partnership between the Parties"].
```

---

## Section 4: Mutual Confidentiality Obligations

```
Each Party, when acting as a Receiving Party, must:

(a) Keep all Confidential Information strictly confidential;

(b) Use Confidential Information only for the Purpose;

(c) Not disclose Confidential Information to anyone except its Representatives who:
    (i) need to know the information for the Purpose; and
    (ii) are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as this Agreement;

(d) Protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information, and in any event no less than reasonable care;

(e) Promptly notify the Disclosing Party if it becomes aware of any unauthorized use or disclosure of Confidential Information.
```

---

## Section 5: Exclusions

```
Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

(a) Was publicly available at the time of disclosure, or becomes publicly available after disclosure through no fault of the Receiving Party;

(b) Was already known to the Receiving Party before disclosure, as shown by its written records;

(c) Was independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information;

(d) Was lawfully received from a third party who had no obligation of confidentiality to the Disclosing Party.
```

---

## Section 6: Permitted Disclosures

```
A Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information if:

(a) Required by law, court order, or governmental authority, provided that the Receiving Party:
    (i) gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice (where legally permitted) to allow the Disclosing Party to seek protective measures;
    (ii) discloses only the minimum information legally required; and
    (iii) cooperates with the Disclosing Party's reasonable efforts to obtain confidential treatment;

(b) Made to the Receiving Party's professional legal or financial advisors who are bound by professional duties of confidentiality.
```

---

## Section 7: Term and Duration

```
This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues for [2/3] years, unless terminated earlier by either Party giving [30] days' written notice to the other Party.

The confidentiality obligations in this Agreement will survive termination or expiration for a period of [USER SELECTED: 2/3/5] years.
```

---

## Section 8: Return or Destruction of Information

```
Upon the Disclosing Party's written request, or upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, the Receiving Party must:

(a) Promptly return all Confidential Information (including all copies) to the Disclosing Party; or

(b) Destroy all Confidential Information (including all copies) and confirm destruction in writing within [10] business days.

However, the Receiving Party may retain:
(i) One archival copy for legal compliance purposes, which remains subject to this Agreement; and
(ii) Copies stored in routine backup systems, provided these are not accessed except for backup recovery purposes.
```

---

## Section 9: No Rights Granted

```
This Agreement does not:

(a) Transfer any ownership, license, or other rights in any Confidential Information, intellectual property, or other assets;

(b) Create any obligation on either Party to proceed with any transaction or business relationship;

(c) Prevent either Party from pursuing similar discussions or agreements with third parties;

(d) Restrict either Party from developing products or services that may compete with those of the other Party, provided no Confidential Information is used.
```

---

## Section 10: Remedies

```
Each Party acknowledges that:

(a) Confidential Information is valuable and unique;

(b) Unauthorized disclosure may cause irreparable harm that cannot be adequately compensated by monetary damages alone;

(c) The Disclosing Party is entitled to seek injunctive relief or other equitable remedies, without the need to prove actual damages or post a bond (to the extent permitted by applicable law), in addition to any other remedies available at law.
```

## Section 11: Jurisdiction-Specific Provisions

*Include based on user's jurisdiction selection:*

### California (USA)

```
California-Specific Provisions:

(a) This Agreement is subject to the California Uniform Trade Secrets Act (California Civil Code §§ 3426-3426.11) for trade secret protection.

(b) Nothing in this Agreement is intended to restrict any Party's employees from exercising their rights under California law regarding employee mobility, including but not limited to California Business and Professions Code § 16600.

(c) The Parties acknowledge that California courts may not enforce provisions that are overly broad in scope, duration, or geography.
```

### England & Wales (UK)

```
England & Wales-Specific Provisions:

(a) This Agreement is governed by common law principles of confidentiality as recognized under English law.

(b) The Parties acknowledge that equitable remedies, including injunctions, are available for breach of confidence under English law.

(c) To the extent that any Confidential Information includes personal data as defined under the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018, the Receiving Party must process such data in compliance with applicable data protection laws.

(d) Nothing in this Agreement excludes or limits liability for fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation.
```

### Spain (EU)

```
Spain-Specific Provisions:

(a) This Agreement is subject to Ley 1/2019, de 20 de febrero, de Secretos Empresariales (Law 1/2019 on Trade Secrets), which implements EU Directive 2016/943.

(b) For the avoidance of doubt, "trade secrets" under Spanish law means information that:
    (i) is secret in that it is not generally known or readily accessible;
    (ii) has commercial value because it is secret; and
    (iii) has been subject to reasonable steps to keep it secret.

(c) To the extent that any Confidential Information includes personal data as defined under the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 and the Ley Orgánica 3/2018, de Protección de Datos Personales y garantía de los derechos digitales (LOPDGDD), the Receiving Party must process such data in compliance with applicable data protection laws.

(d) Any ambiguity in this Agreement will be interpreted in accordance with Spanish civil law principles, including Articles 1281-1289 of the Spanish Civil Code.
```

## Section 12: General Provisions

```
12.1 Entire Agreement
This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties regarding its subject matter and supersedes all prior discussions, negotiations, and agreements.

12.2 Amendments
This Agreement may only be amended by a written document signed by both Parties.

12.3 Severability
If any provision of this Agreement is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions will continue in full force and effect. The Parties will negotiate in good faith to replace any invalid provision with a valid provision that achieves the original intent as closely as possible.

12.4 Waiver
A Party's failure to enforce any right under this Agreement does not waive that right or any other right.

12.5 Assignment
Neither Party may assign or transfer this Agreement without the other Party's prior written consent, except to an affiliate or in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all assets.

12.6 Notices
All notices under this Agreement must be in writing and delivered to the addresses set out above (or such other address as a Party may notify in writing). Notices are effective upon receipt.

12.7 Counterparts
This Agreement may be signed in counterparts, including electronic counterparts, each of which is an original and all of which together form one agreement.
```

## Section 13: Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

*Adapt based on user's jurisdiction and dispute resolution selection:*

### Single Jurisdiction - Courts

```
13.1 Governing Law
This Agreement is governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of [JURISDICTION].

13.2 Jurisdiction
The courts of [JURISDICTION] have exclusive jurisdiction over any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement.
```

### Single Jurisdiction - Arbitration

```
13.1 Governing Law
This Agreement is governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of [JURISDICTION].

13.2 Arbitration
Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement will be finally resolved by arbitration under the [ARBITRATION RULES - e.g., ICC Rules / LCIA Rules / JAMS Rules] by [one/three] arbitrator(s) appointed in accordance with those rules. The seat of arbitration will be [CITY]. The language of arbitration will be English.
```

### Multiple Jurisdictions

```
13.1 Governing Law
This Agreement is governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of [PRIMARY JURISDICTION - typically where the Disclosing Party is based, or as agreed].

13.2 Jurisdiction
[COURTS OPTION]: The courts of [PRIMARY JURISDICTION] have non-exclusive jurisdiction over any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement. Each Party submits to the jurisdiction of those courts.

[ARBITRATION OPTION]: Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement will be finally resolved by arbitration under the ICC Rules of Arbitration by one arbitrator appointed in accordance with those rules. The seat of arbitration will be [NEUTRAL LOCATION, e.g., London / Paris / Geneva]. The language of arbitration will be English.
```

### Mediation First Option

```
13.3 Mediation
Before initiating [litigation/arbitration], the Parties will attempt to resolve any dispute through mediation. Either Party may initiate mediation by written notice to the other. The Parties will select a mutually acceptable mediator within [14] days. If mediation does not resolve the dispute within [30] days of the mediator's appointment, either Party may proceed to [litigation/arbitration].
```
